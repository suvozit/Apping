about     projects     vita     bio     students    

As digital information becomes increasingly cheap and
ubiquitous, how will we keep abreast with the rising tide of
data? Our research group investigates the perceptual,
cognitive, and social factors involved in making sense of
large data collections, and develops novel interactive
systems for visual analysis and communication.
latest + greatest
A TimeNet is a new visualization technique for telling family stories with genealogical data. Presented at AVI 2010 in Rome.
Our work using Mechanical Turk to reliably crowdsource graphical perception studies is an ACM CHI 2010 Best Paper Nominee.
Our research on the perception of time-series visualizations received an ACM CHI 2009 Best Paper Award.
I recently presented A Brief History of Data Visualization at the Stanford HCI seminar. A video is on the internets, as is an earlier talk on interactive visualization.
